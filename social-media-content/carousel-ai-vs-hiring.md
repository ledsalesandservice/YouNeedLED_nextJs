## Slide 2 — Option A: Hire a Receptionist
**Cost:** ~$3,000–$4,500/month (salary + payroll taxes + benefits)
- Covers ~40 hours/week — **not** nights, weekends, or holidays
- Sick days, vacation, turnover, training
- Handles one call at a time
- Great for in-person feel, but expensive and capacity-limited

**Visual:** Person at a desk, clock showing 9–5, "$36K–$54K/year" stamp.

---

## Slide 3 — Option B: Traditional Answering Service
**Cost:** ~$300–$1,200/month (per-minute or per-call pricing)
- 24/7 coverage — but often generic, off-script operators
- Reads from a basic message pad; limited job knowledge
- Usually just **takes a message** — doesn't book or qualify
- Overage fees during busy months; you still call the lead back

**Visual:** Headset operator, sticky-note messages piling up, "still have to call them back" caption.

---

## Slide 4 — Option C: AI Voice Agent
**Cost:** Flat monthly rate — a fraction of a hire
- **24/7/365** — never sick, never on break, never misses a call
- Handles **multiple calls at once**
- Books appointments, qualifies leads, and answers common questions
- Drops a **structured ticket into your CRM** automatically

**Visual:** Clean AI/soundwave graphic → arrow → CRM ticket card populating.
